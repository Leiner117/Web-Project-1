Colosseum

The Flavian Amphitheater, better known as the Colosseum, is one of the most iconic landmarks of Ancient Rome. Built in the 1st century AD, it hosted gladiator fights, public spectacles, and remains one of the most visited monuments in the world.

Opening Hours:

8:30 AM – 7:00 PM (varies by season)

Entry Fee:

€16 (includes access to the Roman Forum)

Official Website:

colosseo.it

Roman Forum

Opening Hours:

8:30 AM – 7:00 PM

Entry Fee:

Included with Colosseum ticket

Website:

Borghese Gallery

Housed in the Villa Borghese, this elegant museum features a world-renowned collection of sculptures and paintings by Bernini, Caravaggio, Raphael, and Titian.

Opening Hours:

Tuesday – Sunday: 9:00 AM – 7:00 PM (Closed on Mondays)

Entry Fee:

€13 standard, €2 reservation fee required

Website:

galleriaborghese.beniculturali.it

Galleria Doria Pamphilj

A hidden gem in the heart of Rome, the Doria Pamphilj Gallery houses a stunning private collection of Baroque art, including works by Velázquez, Caravaggio, and Titian, all within a lavish historic palace.

Opening Hours:

Every day: 9:00 AM – 7:00 PM

Entry Fee:

€14 standard ticket

Website:

doriapamphilj.it

Baths of Caracalla

One of the largest and best-preserved ancient thermal complexes in Rome, the Baths of Caracalla offer a glimpse into the grandeur of Roman public baths, with vast ruins, mosaics, and underground areas.

Opening Hours:

Tuesday – Sunday: 9:00 AM – 6:30 PM (Closed on Mondays)

Entry Fee:

€8 standard, reduced rates available

Website:

coopculture.it

Domus Aurea

The Domus Aurea, or "Golden House", was Emperor Nero’s opulent palace complex, featuring vast halls, frescoes, and innovative architecture that still inspires awe. Guided tours take you underground to explore this fascinating archaeological site.

Opening Hours:

Only on weekends with guided tours (10:00 AM – 4:00 PM)

Entry Fee:

€16 standard ticket

Website:

coopculture.it
